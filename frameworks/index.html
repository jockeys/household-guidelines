The Counting Flowchart

Follow this simple, calm process to address "Stop" behaviors without arguing.

1

Child starts "Stop" behavior.

Parent calmly says, "That's 1." No emotion.

↴
2

Behavior continues.

Parent calmly says, "That's 2." No lecture.

↴
3

Behavior persists.

Parent says, "That's 3. Time-out." Consequence is automatic.

The Family Meeting Agenda

This structured process turns conflict resolution into a collaborative, skill-building exercise.

  • ★
    Start with Compliments: Build connection by having each person appreciate another.
  • ✎
    Review Agenda: Discuss problems added by family members during the week.
  • 💡
    Brainstorm Solutions: Generate ideas without judgment. All suggestions are written down.
  • 👍
    Choose One Solution: Agree on one respectful, reasonable, and helpful solution to try for the week.
  • 🎉
    Plan Family Fun: End on a high note by scheduling an enjoyable activity together.

The Consequence Funnel: From Problem to Lesson

This process turns mistakes into powerful learning opportunities while preserving the parent-child relationship.

CHILD CREATES A PROBLEM

e.g., Forgets homework, breaks a toy, makes a mess.

PARENT RESPONDS WITH EMPATHY

"Oh man, that is so sad." or "Bummer."

PARENT HANDS PROBLEM BACK

"What are you going to do?" or "How do you plan to solve this?"

CONSEQUENCE BECOMES THE TEACHER

The child learns responsibility from the logical or natural outcome, not from parental anger.
